I built AI safety infrastructure because I was tired of hallucinated facts, premature resolution of ambiguity, fabricated citations, and unsafe responses to medical and high-risk queries.

Large language models are optimized for fluent output. They are not optimized for admissibility. When ambiguity is forced into certainty, or when insufficient evidence still produces a confident answer, the system crosses a boundary it should not cross.

I built the layer that determines whether a decision is permitted at all. The system preserves ambiguity when required, refuses when evidence is insufficient, and emits deterministic audit records for every governed outcome.

Accountability belongs to the decision-maker. Governance must be structural, enforceable, and provable.
